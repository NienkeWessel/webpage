Student representation

Dutch education programs are regularly evaluated by an external panel on their quality, as per Dutch law. All such panels contain a student to provide the student perspective. I was part of the panel for the evaluation of three existing and five newly developed Computer Science programs in the Netherlands. Tasks consisted of:

  • Reading preparational documents such as self evaluation reports
  • Drafting up preliminary questions
  • Asking those questions to board, teachers and students of that program
  • Giving feedback on the report compiled by the panel secretary

In this panel, I especially learned on being critical but constructive, as these panels should have the "critical friend" attitude in their work.

As Faculty Assessor, it is my task to give the student perspective on current issues within our faculty, for example during Faculty Board meetings.
